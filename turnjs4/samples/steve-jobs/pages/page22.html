Pixel stood in the middle, their lens spinning fast,
They’d found the great secret of doggies at last!
On their left stood a Great Dane, as big as a horse,
On their right was a Yorkie, a dog, too, of course!

They were different as mountains and pebbles can be,
One was huge as a house, one was small as a flea.
But Pixel’s Dog Map gave a satisfied hum,
"I’ve solved the big riddle! I’m no longer glum!"

"If it has a wet nose, and a tail, and four paws,
And it 'woofs' at the mailman (for no active cause),
Then it fits in my map, it fits in my plan.
It’s a Dog, whether tiny or taller than man!"
